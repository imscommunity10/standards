IMS Captain Roles & Responsibilities

Serving as a bridge between the IMS Core Team and Team Leaders, ensuring seamless communication and effective implementation of IMS initiatives.

Overview

The IMS Captain serves as a managerial representative, responsible for conveying and carrying out official IMS tasks, projects, activities, and announcements to IMS Team Leaders (TLs). Acting as a bridge between the IMS Core Team and TLs, the Captain ensures seamless communication and effective implementation of IMS initiatives as outlined in the IMS framework. Captains operate under the leadership of the IMS Community Head and are integral members of the IMS Captains group.

Essential Skills Required

  • Effective decision-making skills
  • Leadership and management capabilities aligned with the mission of Islam
  • Teamwork and collaboration within the IMS TLs and Captains
  • Emotional intelligence to understand group dynamics and address TL concerns
  • Ability to comprehend, internalize, and communicate the message of Islam effectively
  • Clear and focused communication of IMS vision and updates to TLs

Core Responsibilities

  • Clearly explain the IMS mission, vision, policies, and guidelines to all TLs
  • Foster respectful and insightful discussions on Quran, Seerah, and skill-building topics
  • Prevent Fiqh debates in the TLs and Captains group to maintain harmony
  • Prioritize IMS tasks and ensure timely execution
  • Ensure no TL is manually added; follow the official onboarding procedure
  • Uphold IMS’s Zero Tolerance Policy and act on violations appropriately
  • Actively engage in the Captains group by addressing feedback and suggestions from TLs
  • Support the growth and development of TLs into future IMS Captains
  • Maintain a list of training methods, techniques, and resources in the Captains group

Operational Guidelines

Group Management

  • Ensure your name and phone number are listed in your group’s description
  • Example: Co-Captain: Brother Ahmad – +92345xxxxxxx
  • Do not remove TLs directly; in case of issues, lock the group and inform the Community Head (CH) for final action

Team Coordination and Strategy

  • Conduct strategic planning discussions in:
    • Your own group
    • Captains group
    • Coordinators group
  • Co-captain should Coordinate with Captain before implementing plans
  • Co-captain should represent the group in meetings if the Captain is unavailable
  • Share meeting minutes in your group for transparency

Handling Inactive or Exiting Members

  • If a TL leaves the group:
    • A Captain or Co-Captain of the same gender should follow up
    • Male Co-Captains must only reach out to male TLs
    • Request constructive feedback or reason for leaving
    • Keep a record only if a response is received (no need to log unresponsive cases)

Pre-Session Engagement and Guidance

  • Before any Seerah session or community activity:
    • Tag all TLs in the group
    • Send personal reminders to ensure participation
    • Provide clear guidance if TLs have questions about tasks

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

  • Attend mandatory weekly meetings with the Community Head (CH)
  • Inform in advance if unable to attend a scheduled meeting
  • Submit a written explanation for missing three consecutive internal meetings
  • Appoint at least two Coordinators (one male, one female) in each group
  • Complete and deliver all assigned tasks before the stated deadlines
  • Ensure proper implementation of the Zero-Tolerance Policy in all groups

Leave and Duty Management

  • Fulfill all responsibilities with seriousness and ownership
  • Follow instructions and task management protocols from the IMS Core Team
  • Inform the CH prior to taking leave and ensure a proper handover of duties
  • Re-inform the CH if the leave extends beyond the initial period
  • Notify the CH if unable to return to the role
  • Submit a formal resignation at least one week in advance
  • Use the notice period to complete a proper handover process

Policies

Zero Tolerance Policy: Implementation

  • Understand and enforce the IMS Zero Tolerance Policy
  • If a TL violates the policy:
    • First Violation: Delete the message and share the policy privately only if the TL's message has not been seen by many and no discussion has started. Otherwise, proceed directly to the second violation step
    • Second Violation: Lock the chat, send the policy in the group, and issue a warning privately
    • Third Violation: Lock the chat and report the situation to the Community Head

Impersonation and Misconduct Policy

  • Any impersonation of IMS roles or activities will result in immediate removal
  • Spreading misinformation or making false accusations on behalf of IMS is prohibited
  • Any form of misconduct or defamation against an IMS member will be dealt with strictly to preserve integrity and discipline
